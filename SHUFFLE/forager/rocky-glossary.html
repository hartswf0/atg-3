Rocky Forager · Research Prospectus

Proposed implementation of the COURAGE research agenda.
Status: Implementation Phase · December 2025

Rocky Forager is designed to operationalize the "messy middle" of the creative process. While traditional tools enforce rigid schemas early in the workflow, Rocky Forager functions as a "mnemotechnic" workspace that supports emergent structure.

This system bridges the gap between foraging (information collection) and synthesis (formal writing) by treating text not as a stream of characters, but as manipulable "material" within a spatial environment.

2. System Ontology (Objects)

Defined by six atomic types, designed to separate the source of information from its manipulation.

F

Fragment

The atomic unit of thought. A single line or block of text extracted from a Source.

Context: "Separation of symbol and underlying content" (Spatial Hypertext). Immutable content, mutable arrangement.
T

Tray

The volatile buffer where Fragments are held, reordered, and annotated.

Context: "Emergent structure" — a liminal space delaying formal categorization.
S

Source

The ground truth document. A resource to be mined, remaining pristine and untouched.

N

Note

Marginalia attached to a Fragment. The "connective tissue" binding disjointed ideas.

P

Provenance

The indelible link back to the origin (Title, Line #).

Context: "Orthothetic instrument" (Stiegler). Preserves the exact trace of the past.
K

Frankenstein

The final synthesis. A new document stitched together from Fragments, Notes, and Provenance.

3. Morphisms (Mechanics)

Structure-preserving maps between the ontology types.

Extracts : Creator × Source → Fragment

The act of selecting a line. The Creator mines the Source to generate a Fragment, explicitly preserving its Content and Provenance.

Accumulates : Tray → {Fragment}

The Tray accumulates Fragments into a sequence. It acts as a localized "Ring Memory" for ideas, holding them in a "ready-to-hand" state.

Annotates : Creator × Fragment → Note

The Creator attaches new meaning to a Fragment. This transforms it from raw "data" to interpreted "insight."

Synthesizes : Tray → Frankenstein

The Tray's state is serialized and exported as a new Document, freezing the spatial arrangement into a new linear Artifact.

4. Pattern Glossary

Anti-Tool

The interface minimizes administrative overhead. Clicking a line is the action. No wizards or modes.

Theoretical Connection: Heidegger’s Zuhandenheit (Readiness-to-Hand). The tool should withdraw from attention, becoming an extension of the hand, only becoming "visible" (Present-at-Hand) when it breaks down.

Material-Text

Treating text not as a stream of characters, but as manipulable "bricks" that can be grasped, moved, and stacked.

Theoretical Connection: Reification (Beaudouin-Lafon). Concepts are turned into objects, allowing "Instrumental Interaction" where users manipulate the object directly rather than issuing commands.

Lazy-Synthesis

Separating the "Foraging" phase (collection) from the "Synthesizing" phase (writing).

Theoretical Connection: Emergent Structure (Shipman & Marshall). Users resist formalizing relationships early. Systems must allow structure to remain implicit until the user is ready to "reify" it.

Deep-Provenance

Automated tracking of origin for every piece of data. Exports automatically cite their source.

Theoretical Connection: Tertiary Retention (Stiegler). Externalizing memory to record context of acquisition, fighting "proletarization" (loss of knowledge).
